Hagenauer behavior pattern with subcontractors

Cross-case summary of the 12 months before Hagenauer's own bankruptcy
Director Report · Trend Summary

Across eight subcontractors on the Riemergasse 7 project, Hagenauer shows one consistent pattern in the lead-up to its own bankruptcy: paying subs out of Brisen's cost-plus account in ways that drained Brisen's right to recover the money back.

The trend in three sentences

Hagenauer was the financially distressed party from at least 24 February 2025, when it sent Brisen a demand letter over €4.39 million in unpaid progress invoices and threatened to stop work. Every subcontractor decision after that date was driven by Hagenauer's own liquidity, not by the project.

The pattern shows four expressions: (a) Hagenauer kept paying Daci for four months after Daci's own bankruptcy was opened; (b) Hagenauer over-paid S&K by €1.57 million heading into S&K's bankruptcy — Hagenauer's own lawyer admits this; (c) Hagenauer signed a global waiver with Eybel that extinguished Brisen's €307,580 recharge right even though Eybel itself stayed solvent; (d) Hagenauer drew €159,810 in HHS bond guarantees and absorbed €273,862 in substitute-contractor costs without ever crediting Brisen.

The unifying mechanism is simple. Hagenauer controlled the recovery path at the moment recovery was still available, and chose not to use it. Brisen funded the cost each month under the cost-plus contract.

The eight cases

Case 1
Daci Projektentwicklung GmbH
Crane subcontractor
Bankruptcy confirmed

Bankruptcy opened 19 February 2025 at Vienna Commercial Court, file 7 S 20/25. Bankruptcy administrator not yet public.

The damning fact: Hagenauer kept using Daci as crane sub until ~26 June 2025 — four months after Daci's bankruptcy opened. Brisen reimbursed Hagenauer monthly throughout under the cost-plus account.

Daci's bankruptcy administrator has a clean clawback path against Hagenauer for the four-month payment window. Hagenauer was paying an insolvent sub with Brisen's money.

Source: CM-1 external register check #1246. Public records: wirtschaft.at, evi.gv.at, firmenbuch.ai.
Case 2
S&K Gebäudetechnik GmbH
Electrical / mechanical / HVAC
Bankruptcy confirmed

Bankruptcy opened 11 November 2025 at Vienna Commercial Court, file 28 S 170/25s. Administrator: Dr. Christian Bachmann (Bachmann & Bachmann, Opernring 8, 1010 Vienna). 66 creditors, ~€6.2M liabilities, 23 employees.

The smoking gun: Hagenauer's own lawyers Pochmarski Kober wrote to the administrator on 2 December 2025 admitting Hagenauer had over-paid S&K by €1,569,484, plus €475,052 still outstanding on S&K invoices.

Hagenauer ran a direct-payment scheme to S&K throughout the 60 days before bankruptcy. Internal documents show Hagenauer knew of S&K's liquidity problems from mid-August 2025.

Source: CM-4 ship #1208, CM-1 ship #1246.
Case 3
Gaspar Bau GmbH
Reinforced concrete
Bankruptcy confirmed

Bankruptcy opened 12 June 2024 at Vienna Commercial Court, file 5 S 116/24d. Administrator: Dr. Susi Pariasek. Distribution hearing likely already held (23 months elapsed).

Related-party flag: Gaspar Bau is registered at the same address as Hagenauer itself — Franziskanerplatz 3/11, 1010 Vienna. This may trigger the extended 2-year clawback window for related-party transactions.

Gaspar was replaced via a three-tier chain (Gaspar → HABAU → Hekuri); substitution costs passed through to Brisen under the cost-plus account.

Source: CM-1 ship #1246, unternehmen24.info.
Case 4
Metallbau Eybel GmbH
Locksmith / windows / metalwork
Solvent — waiver case

Not in bankruptcy. Public registers (Northdata, company-register) show Eybel active; 2024 annual financial statement published 8 October 2025 — six days after the alleged waiver.

The damning fact: Hagenauer signed a global settlement agreement with Eybel on 2 October 2025: "all mutual claims declared settled" except 5% warranty. The exact waiver wording is not yet extracted from the 3 candidate PDFs in the document set.

Brisen-side metalwork defect costs that Hagenauer paid out: ~€307,580. The waiver extinguished Brisen's right to recover any of it back from Eybel via Hagenauer's contract chain.

The clawback theory does not apply (Eybel is solvent), but the breach-of-duty-to-preserve-recovery theory does — Hagenauer waived Brisen's recovery channel.

Source: CM-1 ship #1247. Locksmith V2 report claim "slid into insolvency" is unsupported — to be retracted.
Case 5
CB Projekt GmbH
Tiles + natural stone (DE)
Solvent — terminated

Not in bankruptcy. 2024 annual financial statement published 9 February 2026.

Hagenauer terminated CB on 19 May 2025 for contractual default (Austrian Civil Code § 918) — not insolvency. Litigation at Vienna Commercial Court ongoing through 24 November 2025.

Pattern relevance: Hagenauer's litigation theory ("no timely defect notice = no claim") risks blocking Brisen's own defect recovery symmetrically. Different shape from the waiver / over-payment pattern but worth flagging.

Source: CM-1 ship #1246.
Case 6
Hotelhandwerkerservice GmbH (HHS)
Painter (DE)
Solvent — counter-pattern

Not in bankruptcy. HHS is the plaintiff suing Hagenauer at Vienna Commercial Court (file 12 Cg 129/25b) for €234,752 unpaid contractor fees plus €255,121 bond-draw recovery.

The counter-pattern (Hagenauer as distressed payor):

1. February 2025 — Hagenauer demand letter to Brisen, €4.39M unpaid, threat to stop work.

2. May 2025 — HHS terminates Hagenauer for material payment default.

3. July 2025 — Hagenauer draws two HHS performance bonds totalling €159,810. No document shows the proceeds credited to Brisen.

4. Hagenauer asserts €273,862 in substitute-contractor costs against HHS — no recovery passed back to Brisen.

5. Hagenauer commissions REKO as replacement painter at €68.50/h; 21 October 2025 bills €811,642 (€172,774 unpaid). All passed via cost-plus to Brisen, no recovery credit.

Source: CM-1 ship #1226.
Case 7
Zottmann Restaurierungswerkstatt GmbH
Stone restoration
Solvent — inverse

Not in bankruptcy. Active.

Hagenauer sent Zottmann a cure-period notice on 21 July 2025 (Austrian Civil Code §§ 918 + 1168) for performance default — resolved without bankruptcy.

Inverse direction: Zottmann is itself a €1,012,038 creditor of Hagenauer (Klema demand letter, 27 October 2025).

Low pattern relevance. Probably drops out of the claim. Worth retaining as pattern evidence of Hagenauer's distressed creditor stack.

Source: CM-2 ship #1206, CM-1 ship #1246.
Case 8
eden-real bau- & immobilientreuhand GmbH
Plastering
Solvent — routine swap

Not in bankruptcy. Annual filings regular through fiscal year 2024.

Replaced mid-project by My Hansa Bausanierung. Contractual sub-swap; no distress signal.

Likely drops out of the claim. Listed here for completeness.

Source: CM-1 ship #1246.
