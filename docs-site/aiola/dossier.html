Section 1 · The Company

What aiOla Actually Is

aiOla is a deep-tech voice and conversational AI lab building speech-to-workflow agents for frontline enterprise operations. Workers speak naturally in noisy, multilingual, jargon-heavy environments — kitchens, factory floors, construction sites, airline maintenance hangars — and aiOla converts that speech into structured, auditable data and triggers downstream workflows in real time.

Section 7 · The Three Proposals

What Amir Is Selling — And How To Position Each

N1Use Cases for Brisen Operations Genuinely Interesting

Posture: Real operational fit at Mandarin Oriental Vienna (housekeeping, F&B, multilingual staff, maintenance, concierge) and on European construction sites (ClaimsMax / SiteWatch evidence capture). Frame as commercial pilot — not investment-conditional.

Talking points:

  • MO Vienna is the obvious European reference deployment — multilingual staff, noisy F&B environments, structured ops data missing today.
  • Brisen owns the European hotel-owner relationships. That's the scarce asset. Their tech is ours to deploy, ours to test.
  • 90-day pilot, single asset, defined KPIs. They get the logo + case study; we get the integration without equity strings.

Sharp Questions — N1

  1. Show me 2 reference customers in noisy / multilingual environments. Names, contract size, time-on-platform. Can I speak to one?
  2. What's your German + Russian + Polish + English accuracy in a real hotel back-of-house recording (not lab)? Send me a benchmark file we can A/B test.
  3. Pilot cost — who pays? Who owns the data? What's the exit clause if accuracy disappoints?
  4. On-site latency with intermittent connectivity — does it work offline-first or cloud-only?
  5. Are you in active conversation with MOHG or any other luxury operator? If yes, who, and at what stage?
N2Hospitality Vertical / NVIDIA Co-Marketing Cautiously Open

Posture: Useful for the Peter Storer (NVIDIA) ecosystem narrative — three complementary AI companies serving European hospitality (Brisen AI intelligence + aiOla voice + Bellboy Robotics physical). Marketing partnership, not structural. No exclusivity, no equity.

Talking points:

  • Coordinate the NVIDIA narrative — when we follow up with Peter Storer, the aiOla conversation is evidence the ecosystem is forming organically.
  • Brisen owns European hotel-owner relationships; aiOla brings voice; Bellboy brings robotics. Jointly we tell a story Peter wants to tell internally.
  • No exclusivity. Both sides can pursue whoever they want.

Sharp Questions — N2

  1. How many hotel customers do you have today? Names, PMS integration depth, ARR per customer?
  2. Are you in active conversation with MOHG, Kempinski, Six Senses, Mandarin, or any of our origination targets? Who, what stage?
  3. Do you expect exclusivity in European hospitality — and if so, what do you offer in exchange?
  4. Have you signed anything with Bellboy Robotics, or is that still NVIDIA-side coordination?
  5. What is NVIDIA's actual ask of you in Inception? Are they pushing a portfolio bundle, or are you self-organising the ecosystem?
N3$10M SAFE / ~13% / "Repricing" / Time-Sensitive Surgical Questioning

Posture: Polite. Curious. The Garber + Rowan + United signal is too strong to dismiss — but we ask institutional-grade VC questions that any clean deal answers in five minutes. We don't refuse. We surface the data and let the round's own facts dictate the answer.

Talking points to demote N3 conversationally:

  • "Brisen is a real-estate operator, not a venture Voice-AI LP. A $10M cheque has to clear an institutional process here, and we'd be a strange fit on your cap table."
  • "We're happy to be a strategic partner without the equity — partnership shouldn't be capital-conditional."
  • "If there's a strategic rationale for a smaller token cheque tied to a signed pilot, we can look. A $10M anchor on a SAFE with re-pricing is not us as proposed."

Sharp Questions — N3 (the surgical set)

On the round itself
  1. Why a SAFE after two priced equity rounds? SAFEs typically precede priced rounds — what triggered this structure?
  2. What was the price-per-share of the last priced round, and what is this SAFE's cap? What's the % delta? Is there a discount on top of the cap?
  3. Who is the price-setting lead investor at the new cap, and what's their cheque size?
  4. Are existing investors (United, New Era, Hamilton Lane, UST) taking pro-rata at this cap? Which ones are stepping up, which ones are passing — and why?
  5. What's the MFN clause structure? Liquidation preferences inherited or stripped at conversion?
On the financials
  1. ARR today (booked, recognised — not pipeline)? Net-new ARR last 12 months? Net revenue retention? Gross logo churn?
  2. Cash on hand today, monthly burn, runway in months at current burn?
  3. Top-5 customer concentration as % of ARR? Average contract length and renewal rate?
  4. What does the $10M specifically fund — sales hires, R&D, GTM expansion, working capital?
On the moat
  1. If Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google ships native speech-to-workflow in the next 12–18 months, what's left of your defensible position?
  2. What do your patents actually claim — algorithmic, data-pipeline, or use-case specific? Have any been challenged?
  3. Drax is open-source. Why give that away — what's the strategic calculus, and does it commoditise your core?
On strategic rationale (the uncomfortable one)
  1. Mitch, Marc Rowan, and United are already on the cap table. Why is a $10M strategic check from a European real-estate / hospitality operator the next piece — and would a smaller anchor ($1–2M) tied to a signed MO Vienna deployment work for the round, or do you specifically need the full $10M from one source?
  2. Why not strategic capital from Salesforce, Microsoft, NVIDIA Ventures, or a hospitality strategic like Accor or Marriott Ventures?
  3. What's the governance ask at $10M / 13% — observer seat, board seat, none?

How To Play This Meeting

  1. Open on N1. "We see a real fit at Mandarin Oriental Vienna." Establishes Brisen as a real operator, not a passive investor pretending interest. Move to N1 sharp questions.
  2. Move to N2. "We can co-write the European hospitality + NVIDIA narrative." Show willingness to co-market, withhold exclusivity. Press on existing hotel customers.
  3. Let Amir bring up N3. When he does, deliver the surgical questions — particularly Q1 (why SAFE after priced rounds), Q4 (existing investors taking pro-rata), Q6 (real ARR / NRR / churn), Q13 (why Brisen specifically as the next $10M).
  4. If answers are clean → propose a $1–2M anchor tied to a signed MO Vienna deployment, conditional on Sergey financial review.
  5. If answers are evasive → "We'll need a full data room before we can take this internally," meeting ends, ball in their court, round closes without us. The exit is graceful and the partnership is preserved.

The exit line if pushed: "Capital allocation at Brisen runs through a real-estate IC — a $10M Voice-AI SAFE is structurally outside our mandate. We'd rather earn our way in through the pilot."
