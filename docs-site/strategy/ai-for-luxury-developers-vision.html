Brisen AI — Strategic Vision 2026

The operating system of luxury real estate development.

Built by an owner-developer who has lived every phase as every counterparty — on a EUR 250M Mandarin Oriental project, not in a lab.

The thesis: luxury real estate development is a five-year, multi-counterparty, multi-million-document journey. The owner is the only party that sees all five phases. Every phase has proprietary data locked in PDFs, emails and one-off spreadsheets. AI converts that data into money, time and decisions — phase by phase, counterparty by counterparty. Brisen is the only operator that has been on every side of the table and can build the platform that knows it.

01

Why this category does not yet exist

The concrete gap

Luxury hotel development is a EUR 50B annual market in Europe alone with essentially zero AI penetration — 17% in US hospitality, materially less in EU luxury. The reasons are structural and they are also the reasons Brisen wins this category:

Proprietary data is locked

Brand standards, tender history, claims, design iterations all sit inside family-owned developer groups. Generic AI cannot reach it.

Domain too complex for horizontal tools

Brand standards × local building code × operator constraints × buyer expectations = no off-the-shelf SaaS handles it.

Decision-makers trust only builders

Owners trust people who have built what they are building. Tech vendors selling cold to owners get nowhere.

Pain is real and recurring

Subcontractor insolvency, claim disputes, buyer handover gaps, brand-standard misalignment — every project pays the same tax.EUR 6M+ identified by AI on one EUR 250M project · 4.6% recovery

The five-phase journey

Where AI plugs in

Every luxury hotel or branded-residence project moves through five phases. Each phase has structured-but-unstructured data, named owner pains, and a concrete AI capability that converts pain into outcome. Phase 4 is production today. The other four are the platform's growth path.

01

Land + Concept

Capital, comp-sets, off-market signal
Months 0-6
Designed
The pain
Underwriting a EUR 100M+ luxury asset today means 8-12 weeks of analyst time on Excel, comparables hunted by phone, and a capital stack iterated three times because the operator changed the residence mix. By the time the model is closed, the deal moved or the macro shifted.
Capital-Stack Optimizer
Days, not months

Takes operator constraints (residence mix, F&B program, brand fees) + bank covenants + macro luxury-demand inputs. Outputs optimal debt / mezz / equity blend per phase, sensitised to operator and lender side.

How MOHG / Corinthia / Aman fee schedules + Aukera / sparkasse / private-bank term-sheet patterns + Brisen's own closed-deal data, behind a constraint solver.
Financial close in days · ready for committee
Site IRR Engine
One-click sensitivity

Integrates global comp-set ADRs, branded-residence prices per market (Vienna 25-30k/sqm, Dubai 40k+, Geneva private-bank-driven), operator fee structures and local construction-cost indices into a single IRR model that responds in real time.

How Curated luxury-asset transaction database + STR/HVS-style comp scrape + construction-cost feed + Brisen's own actuals as ground truth.
Re-underwrite a target in 20 minutes
Off-Market Deal Radar
Hear it first

Scrapes broker letters, distress filings, succession events, planning notices. Cross-references against Brisen's relationship graph: which counterparty knows which seller, which advisor is closest.

How Public registry + insolvency gazette + Land Registry + Brisen contact graph, fused via signal classifier into a ranked target list.
Solve "we heard about it too late"
02

Design + Entitlement

Brand standards, code conflicts, permit timing
Months 6-18
Designed
The pain
A 1,500-page brand manual interpreted by a team of consultants over six months. Brand standards collide with local code on month seven. A permit slip of three months breaks the financing waterfall. Each iteration costs six-figure design rework.
Brand-Standards Translator
From manual to tender-grade spec

Ingests the full brand book (MOHG, Corinthia, Aman, Six Senses). Outputs procurement-grade tender specifications, design briefs, FF&E schedules and operational manuals — all cross-referenced and version-controlled.

How Brand manual ingestion + Brisen's MO Vienna interpretation library as ground truth + retrieval-augmented generation tuned to procurement language.
6-month cycle reduced to weeks
Operator-vs-Code Conflict Detector
Kill conflicts before architecture

Runs brand standards against Austrian Bauordnung, German LBO, Dubai Civil Defence, Swiss cantonal codes. Flags every conflict before the architect drafts a sheet — the most expensive class of re-design avoided.

How Code corpora per jurisdiction + brand-standard parsed claims, cross-referenced via structured rule extraction.
Stop rework before drawings are issued
Permit Timeline Simulator
Realistic, not optimistic

Trained on actual permit cycles across Magistrat Wien, Bauamt Baden-Baden, Geneva DCTI, Dubai DDA. Gives realistic forecasts not the developer's wish — feeds capital-stack timing and operator commitment.

How Permit dataset (public + Brisen actuals) trained against project-type + complexity vectors; Monte Carlo over historical variance.
Capital-stack timing that survives reality
03

Tender + Procurement

Bids, normalisation, supplier risk
Months 12-24
Designed
The pain
Only 10-20% of LOIs convert to executed contracts (named in our own 15-Mar strategy session). Bidders price different scopes. Comparing them is six weeks of QS time. Half the suppliers who win are not the ones who should — sometimes catastrophically (Hagenauer).
TenderMax
Brand-standards in, bid packages out

Translates brand-standard outputs into full bid packages. Normalises returned bids across contractors (different scopes, different assumptions, different exclusions) into apples-to-apples comparison. Recommends optimal supplier mix for the project envelope.

How Spec library + bid-decomposition LLM + cost-database trained on Brisen + EU luxury comparables.
LOI conversion from 10-20% to 40%+
Supplier Risk Profiler
Catch the next Hagenauer before contract award

Financial health, prior insolvencies, claims history, management turnover, bank-account changes, gazette filings. A live risk score per bidder — the score that would have flagged Hagenauer 18 months early.

How Insolvency-gazette feed + Bonitätsdatenbank + Brisen's own claims history + pattern classifier on operational drift signals.
Subcontractor insolvency caught upstream
04

Construction

The proven layer · production today
Months 18-48
Production
The pain
50,000+ pages of contracts, variations, invoices, correspondence. Subcontractor insolvency mid-project. Schedules slip silently because contractor self-reports. Change orders are line-by-line battles where the side with better data wins. Brisen lived it on MO Vienna — and built four products to fix it.
ClaimsMax
EUR 6M+ identified on EUR 250M asset · 4.6% recovery

Ingests the full project document stack — contracts, variations, invoices, correspondence, MOHG instructions. Cross-references every change against entitlements. Identifies recoverable claims in 30 days that manual review would take six to twelve months to find.

How Multi-document retrieval + claim-pattern library + entitlement reasoning + cite-everything output that lawyers and arbitrators trust.
EUR 2-5M per EUR 100M construction spend
SiteWatch
Cameras don't lie

Computer vision on continuous site cameras. Compares observed progress against planned schedule. Replaces contractor self-report with objective verification — the most common cost-overrun mechanism in luxury construction.

How Vision models tuned to construction milestones + BIM/programme reference + change-detection over time.
Schedule reality, not contractor narrative
Subcontractor Insolvency Early-Warning
60 days of warning, not 60 days of damage

Detects the Hagenauer pattern as it forms: slow invoices, late site attendance, management resignations, bank-account changes, supplier complaints. Probability score updated weekly with directional alerts.

How Operational drift signals + financial-health overlay + pattern model trained on Brisen's own incident library.
Time to switch contractors before failure
Change-Order Arbitrage Detector
Win every line-item negotiation

For every change order, flags which line has the weakest documentation — i.e. where the contractor will try to price up. Hands the project manager line-by-line negotiation leverage before the meeting starts.

How Document completeness scoring + entitlement-pattern library + benchmark pricing per scope.
3-7% of change-order value retained

OwnerView
Real-time, not quarterly

Portfolio-wide dashboard: cost-per-key, programme compliance, contractor performance, GOP and RevPAR vs comp-set, brand fees earned vs delivered. Real time, owner-controlled, drill-down to line items.

How PMS / SPA / accounting connectors + operator-data sovereignty layer + benchmarking corpus.
Operator accountability the day it slips
Buyer-Dispute Prevention
Catch the Cupial gap before handover

Pre-handover defect AI: ingests punch-walks + brand standards + buyer SPA + Sonderwunsch agreements. Flags every gap that will trigger a dispute — before the buyer's surveyor arrives.

How Image classification on defect categories + SPA + Sonderwunsch parsing + gap detection against entitlement.
Stop EUR 250K+ buyer disputes upstream
Warranty / Gewährleistung Clock
Never lose a claim to a clock

Tracks every defect window for every component across every contract. Automated claim-trigger alert before expiry. Solves the silent loss-of-rights pattern Brisen lived through.

How Contract clause extraction + defect log + jurisdiction-specific deadline rules.
Zero claims lost to deadline
Operator-Performance Arbitrage
Renegotiate or rebrand from data

Benchmarks operator GOP, brand-standard compliance, marketing spend, F&B contribution, BSC scores against comp-set in real time. The data set every renegotiation or rebrand decision should rest on.

How STR / HotStats / Brisen owner-network data + management-contract parsing + variance classifier.

The ecosystem reframe

Same data · every counterparty · sovereign per side

The Apr-9 brainstorm flagged the missing concrete: "network of owners and operators", "data camaraderie, separation, segmentation". The platform is not just owner-side. Every counterparty around the development gets a Brisen-tuned AI assistant on their slice of the data — segmented, permissioned, sovereign. Network effect: each new participant makes the platform smarter for the owner without breaching anyone's data perimeter.

Commercial structure

Three-tier · owner-aligned · pilot-to-platform path

The Researcher inventory flagged the single biggest commercial gap — "post-pilot commercial structure not drafted in any source file." The platform makes the structure obvious: three tiers, ordered by friction and alignment.

Tier 1 · Pilot
Per-Project Licence

One project, one fee, defined scope (typically ClaimsMax + SiteWatch on an active or completed asset). Designed for low procurement friction — Phase-1 entry point for new developer relationships.

Best fit · first engagement
Tier 2 · Platform
Annual Subscription

Owner-portfolio licence across all active developments. All capabilities, all phases, sovereign deployment per group. Predictable spend; platform improves continuously on owner's own data.

Best fit · 3+ active developments
Tier 3 · Aligned
Success Share

Per recovered claim, per cost saved, per dispute prevented. Brisen earns when the owner wins. Highest alignment, longest sales cycle, most defensible margin once landed.

Best fit · institutional owners

Infrastructure

NVIDIA-preferred · sovereign-deployable · vendor-resilient

Three-layer architecture. NVIDIA at the bottom. Sovereign on top.

Brisen runs a three-layer stack — infrastructure, platform, applications. NVIDIA is the preferred infrastructure partner (chips, NIM microservices, NeMo training pipelines) but the platform is engineered infrastructure-agnostic.

Each owner deploys sovereign: on-premise, private cloud, or Brisen-managed. Data never leaves the owner's perimeter. This closes three of the Researcher's flagged gaps simultaneously — procurement security (Topic 10), NVIDIA single-vendor risk (Topic 8), and the data-moat thesis (Topic 5) become one coherent story.
