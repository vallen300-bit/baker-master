Brisen AI — Strategic Vision 2026

The operating system of luxury real estate development.

Built by an owner-developer who has lived every phase as every counterparty — on a EUR 250M Mandarin Oriental project, not in a lab.

The thesis: luxury real estate development is a five-year, multi-counterparty, multi-million-document journey. The owner is the only party that sees all five phases. Every phase has proprietary data locked in PDFs, emails and one-off spreadsheets. AI converts that data into money, time and decisions — phase by phase, counterparty by counterparty. Brisen is the only operator that has been on every side of the table and can build the platform that knows it.
The category framing: the construction-tech incumbents (Trimble, Procore, Autodesk) are arming the GC against the Owner. By 2028 the standard stack will be two-tier: an Execution Layer (Trimble / Procore) that the GC runs, and a Governance Layer — the Owner OS — that the developer runs above them. Brisen builds the Governance Layer.15

01

Why this category does not yet exist

The concrete gap

Europe's hotel pipeline reached 2,180 projects / ~340,000 rooms in Q1 2026 (+14% YoY), with luxury and upper-upscale at record development levels.1 Yet AI penetration in hospitality sits at ~17%2, and on the development side — the multi-year, multi-counterparty journey before a hotel opens — penetration is effectively zero. The reasons are structural; they are also the reasons Brisen wins this category:

Proprietary data is locked

Brand standards, tender history, claims, design iterations all sit inside family-owned developer groups. Generic AI cannot reach it. 80% of construction-AI pilots never reach production — fragmented data is the named cause.3

Domain too complex for horizontal tools

Brand standards × local building code × operator constraints × buyer expectations = no off-the-shelf SaaS handles it. Procore + Autodesk own collaboration; neither sells to the owner-developer of a EUR 250M luxury asset.3

Decision-makers trust only builders

Owners trust people who have built what they are building. Tech vendors selling cold get nowhere. The major-brand AI push (Marriott $1.1B in 2026)4 is operator-side guest experience — no one is doing development-side AI for owners.

Pain is real and quantified

HKA's CRUX Report 8 across 2,200 projects / $2.43T CapEx: average sums in dispute = 33.4% of contract budget; time extensions sought = 65.8% of schedule.5Brisen's MO Vienna recovery: EUR 6M+ on EUR 250M · 4.6% — conservative against industry baseline

02b

Owner · Operator · GC — three AI mandates, one asset

McKinsey-style differentiation15

Every luxury hotel asset has three AI mandates running in parallel. They look similar; they are not. Brisen plays in exactly one of them. The other two are HMA inputs, not Brisen's product.

Layer 1 · Operator
Guest & Revenue AI

Brand standardization, guest acquisition, NOI maximization. Marriott $1.2B 2026 tech spend; MOHG IRIS +54% F&B revenue.4,15

Owner posture Do NOT build this. Underwrite via HMA. Ensure operator's AI flows to NOI without imposing unrecoverable IT CapEx on the asset.
Layer 2 · GC
Execution & Margin AI

Subcontractor coordination, schedule adherence, rework reduction. Procore + Buildots delay reduction up to 50%; Trimble buying Document Crunch April 2026.9,10,15

Owner posture Mandate use in tender terms. Don't pay premium. This is the GC's GMP-protection kit — not the Owner's proprietary alpha.
Layer 3 · OWNER — Brisen
Capital & Lifecycle AI

GDV optimization, CapEx defense, entitlement speed, exit multiples. "AI used to engineer the financial and physical DNA of the asset from Day 0 to Year 10."15

Owner posture Build this. Capital governance weapon. Not a construction tool. Not a hospitality gimmick.

McKinsey estimates generative AI will contribute $110-180B net-new value to real estate. Operators use it for RevPAR. GCs use it for margin. The Owner-Developer uses it to de-risk capital deployment, maximize GDV, and compress cap rates at exit.15

Tender + Procurement

Bids, normalisation, supplier risk
Months 12-24
Designed
The pain
Only 10-20% of LOIs convert to executed contracts (named in our own 15-Mar strategy session). Bidders price different scopes. Comparing them is six weeks of QS time. Half the suppliers who win are not the ones who should — sometimes catastrophically (Hagenauer).
TenderMax
Brand-standards in, bid packages out

Translates brand-standard outputs into full bid packages. Normalises returned bids across contractors (different scopes, different assumptions, different exclusions) into apples-to-apples comparison. Recommends optimal supplier mix for the project envelope.

How Spec library + bid-decomposition LLM + cost-database trained on Brisen + EU luxury comparables.
LOI conversion from 10-20% to 40%+
Supplier Risk Profiler
Catch the next Hagenauer before contract award

Financial health, prior insolvencies, claims history, management turnover, bank-account changes, gazette filings. A live risk score per bidder — the score that would have flagged Hagenauer 18 months early.

How Insolvency-gazette feed + Bonitätsdatenbank + Brisen's own claims history + pattern classifier on operational drift signals.
Subcontractor insolvency caught upstream

Construction

The proven layer · production today
Months 18-48
Production
The pain
50,000+ pages of contracts, variations, invoices, correspondence. Subcontractor insolvency mid-project. Schedules slip silently because contractor self-reports. Change orders are line-by-line battles where the side with better data wins. Brisen lived it on MO Vienna — and built four products to fix it.
ClaimsMax
EUR 6M+ identified on EUR 250M asset · 4.6% recovery

Ingests the full project document stack — contracts, variations, invoices, correspondence, MOHG instructions. Cross-references every change against entitlements. Identifies recoverable claims in 30 days that manual review would take six to twelve months to find. Benchmarked against HKA: industry-average sums-in-dispute are 33.4% of contract budget5 — Brisen's 4.6% recovery is conservative against the dispute baseline. Document Crunch (closest analogue) reports up to 80% contract-review time reduction; Trimble acquired it April 2026 — category is now strategic.9

How Multi-document retrieval + claim-pattern library + entitlement reasoning + cite-everything output that lawyers and arbitrators trust.
EUR 2-5M per EUR 100M construction spend
SiteWatch
Cameras don't lie

Computer vision on continuous site cameras. Compares observed progress against planned schedule. Replaces contractor self-report with objective verification — the most common cost-overrun mechanism in luxury construction. Comparable horizontal players (Buildots $166M raised, Doxel, OpenSpace) document 11-20% schedule acceleration, 38% rework reduction, 10% monthly cash-flow saving, delays caught 2-3 weeks earlier.10 Brisen's version is owner-luxury-specific, not GC-horizontal.

How Vision models tuned to construction milestones + BIM/programme reference + change-detection over time.
Schedule reality, not contractor narrative
Subcontractor Insolvency Early-Warning
60 days of warning, not 60 days of damage

Detects the Hagenauer pattern as it forms: slow invoices, late site attendance, management resignations, bank-account changes, supplier complaints. Probability score updated weekly with directional alerts. Atradius confirms DACH construction insolvencies above pre-Covid by wide margin; Austria peaked 2024 / early 2025; global insolvencies +3% in 2026.11 The pattern Brisen lived through is the live operating environment for every developer in DACH today.

How Operational drift signals + financial-health overlay + pattern model trained on Brisen's own incident library.
Time to switch contractors before failure
Change-Order Arbitrage Detector
Win every line-item negotiation

For every change order, flags which line has the weakest documentation — i.e. where the contractor will try to price up. Hands the project manager line-by-line negotiation leverage before the meeting starts.

How Document completeness scoring + entitlement-pattern library + benchmark pricing per scope.

The Owner Air Gap — 2026-2028 category trajectory

Why the Governance Layer is inevitable15

Trimble's acquisition of Document Crunch (2 April 2026) marks the end of the first wave of horizontal construction-AI point solutions and the start of incumbent consolidation. The strategic conclusion for the ultra-luxury Owner-Developer is not that the category is closed — it is that the incumbents are arming the GC against the Owner. The Owner needs its own AI. Three phases follow.15

Phase 1 · 2026
The Great Horizontal Consolidation
Trimble buys Document Crunch. Autodesk + Procore acquire or build identical embedded AI contract-risk tools. The GC becomes "AI-enabled" as a baseline. Standalone generic AI document startups are acquired or die.
Phase 2 · Mid-2026 to Mid-2027
The Owner "Air Gap" Crisis
Owner-Developers face margin compression during tender + construction. They bring analog legal + QS teams to a digital gunfight against AI-armed GCs. Change-order approval rates spike — GCs use AI to flawlessly justify variations the Owner cannot audit in time.
Phase 3 · Late 2027 to 2028
The Sovereign Owner OS
Tier 1 capital allocators (SWFs, mega-PE RE arms, ultra-luxury owner-developers) fund and adopt bespoke verticalized "Owner Intelligence" platforms. Standard SOP becomes a two-tier stack.

Commercial structure

Three-tier · owner-aligned · pilot-to-platform path

The Researcher inventory flagged the single biggest commercial gap — "post-pilot commercial structure not drafted in any source file." The platform makes the structure obvious: three tiers, ordered by friction and alignment.

Go-to-market — Boardroom UI, not Jobsite UI15,18

Procore won construction by building for the superintendent in the mud and going viral subcontractor-to-subcontractor. Brisen does the opposite: end-user is the Chief Investment Officer, the Development Director, the Operator's VP of Technical Services. UI excels at financial waterfalls, cap-rate compression scenarios, design-yield optimization — not whether drywall went in room 402. GTM is high-touch, white-glove enterprise sales to a concentrated universe of ultra-luxury family offices, sovereign wealth funds, and elite institutional developers — typically brokered through tier-one operators (MOHG, Corinthia, Aman) mandating it as a standard for their owners.

Tier 1 · Pilot
Per-Project Licence

One project, one fee, defined scope (typically ClaimsMax + SiteWatch on an active or completed asset). Designed for low procurement friction — Phase-1 entry point for new developer relationships.

Best fit · first engagement
Tier 2 · Platform
Annual Subscription

Owner-portfolio licence across all active developments. All capabilities, all phases, sovereign deployment per group. Predictable spend; platform improves continuously on owner's own data.

Best fit · 3+ active developments
Tier 3 · Aligned
Success Share

Per recovered claim, per cost saved, per dispute prevented. Brisen earns when the owner wins. Highest alignment, longest sales cycle, most defensible margin once landed.
