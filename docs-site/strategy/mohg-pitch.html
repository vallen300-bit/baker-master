PART 01

The Proposition in One Paragraph

What we are asking — bottom line first

We propose a corporate-level licence extension between Brisen AI Holdings (Geneva) and Mandarin Oriental Hotel Group, granting MOHG access to ClaimsMax — and to the three additional development-side AI products in our roadmap — across the MOHG-operated portfolio. Structure: a contractual extension off the deployment that already operates at MO Vienna, rather than a fresh procurement engagement. Year-1 fee: €80,000. Year-2 onward: usage-based or per-property uplift. Term: rolling 12 months with mutual review.

We are bringing this to the Group because we are owners ourselves, the product is operationally proven on a Mandarin Oriental asset, and the natural next deployment is at the brand level rather than property-by-property. We believe Mandarin Oriental should be the first hotel group at which this capability is institutionalized.

PART 02

What We Have Built — The MO Vienna Proof

Production, not pilot · operational, not theoretical

As MOHG knows, Brisen Group's MO Vienna development encountered a substantial general-contractor dispute, including an insolvency event and a contractor claim in the order of €6 million. The defence of that claim was assembled, document-by-document, with the support of an AI product Brisen developed internally: ClaimsMax.

ClaimsMax ingests the full corpus of a development project — contracts, addenda, invoices, RFIs, change orders, correspondence, expert reports — and produces audit-ready investigation reports identifying every line of the contractor's claim that is unsupported, double-counted, mis-categorized, or contrary to the executed agreement. It is not a research assistant; it is a litigation-grade product.

ClaimsMax has been operating against the Hagenauer claim corpus continuously. External professionals — including external legal counsel — log in directly to view investigation reports. The product is past pilot stage; it is in production, on a real Mandarin Oriental asset, against a real €6M claim, with documented results.

ClaimsMax is the first of four products in a development-side AI suite. The other three are positioned to address the next-most-painful operational moments in any luxury hospitality development:

PART 03

Why Mandarin Oriental — and Why Now

An owner-to-group conversation, not a vendor sales motion

Mandarin Oriental is the right first-Group partner for three reasons that have nothing to do with revenue and everything to do with fit.

  • We are inside the relationship already. MO Vienna runs ClaimsMax today. There is no procurement diligence to perform — the operational evidence sits in the asset. The extension can be a contractual addendum, not a new vendor onboarding.
  • The product was designed against a Mandarin Oriental project. Every product judgement in ClaimsMax was shaped by the contractual structures, operator-developer dynamics, and luxury construction standards present at MO Vienna. It speaks Mandarin Oriental's language because Mandarin Oriental's language is the language it was built in.
  • The window is open. No European competitor at Series A or beyond has built development-side AI for luxury hospitality. The first hotel group to institutionalize this capability owns a reputational position in front of the next decade of brand competition.

We are also conscious of where Mandarin Oriental has already invested in AI — on the brand and guest-experience side, including the Kleitman commentary in early 2026. Our offer is complementary, not competitive: the development-side slot Group has not yet filled.

A note on positioning

This is not a vendor pitch. Brisen Group is a Mandarin Oriental hotel owner. The conversation we are seeking is owner-to-Group, not seller-to-buyer. If the Group sees fit to institutionalize a capability one of its owners has already built and proven, the path is faster than any third-party procurement route would allow.

PART 04

The Proposal · The Ask

Concrete structure · concrete numbers · concrete commitments

What we offer Mandarin Oriental

  • Corporate-level access to ClaimsMax across the MOHG-operated portfolio — properties chosen by MOHG.
  • Priority deployment of TenderMax, OwnerView, and SiteWatch as they become production-ready (Months 6 / 9 / 12).
  • Named Mandarin Oriental advisory seat on the Brisen AI product council — direct input into roadmap priorities.
  • Co-development model on MOHG-specific operational features, with IP shared on a basis to be agreed.
  • No procurement re-diligence — the deal is structured as an extension of the existing MO Vienna operational deployment.

What we ask in return

  • Year-1 licence fee: €80,000 — payable to Brisen AI Holdings (Geneva).
  • 12-month rolling term with mutual review at Month 10.
  • Year-2 structure to be agreed at review: usage-based, per-property uplift, or step-up flat fee.
  • One named MOHG operational sponsor to anchor information flow into the product team.
  • Right to reference the relationship on the Brisen AI investor and partnership materials, subject to MOHG approval of any specific public reference.

The €80,000 Year-1 figure is set deliberately below standard enterprise-AI licensing — this is not the commercial peak of the relationship; it is the entry point. We believe the value to MOHG, even in the first year, will materially exceed the fee, and the structure is designed so that Year-2 economics align with the realized value at review.

Counsel selection, contractual mechanics, and IP-sharing detail can be finalized within four weeks of an indication of interest. We are prepared to share the v1 ClaimsMax investigation report (redacted) from the MO Vienna deployment under NDA so that MOHG can evaluate the product against a real Mandarin Oriental case before signing anything.
