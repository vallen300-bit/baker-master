Market & Opportunity

Why this vertical, why this moment, why no one else has it

Luxury hospitality development is a multi-billion-euro global industry where every single project is hand-managed, every cost line is contested by counterparties, and every operator-developer has been failed by generic ConTech. The slot Brisen AI fills did not exist five years ago and is wide open today.

Segment definition. Luxury and ultra-luxury hospitality development meeting three criteria:

  • Capex threshold — new-build or major-refurbishment with $50M+ project capex
  • Branded operator — operating under (or candidate for) a luxury brand standard
  • Ownership structure — developer-owner separate from operator

Launch geography: Europe. Follow-on geographies: MENA, China, US — pulled by Corinthia and MOHG footprint into those markets.

Why now. Three forces converge in 2026:

  • Construction inflation has made claim defence, tender governance, and budget oversight existentially valuable to luxury developers. Single-digit-percentage savings on a €100M development are €5-10M absolute, line-for-line.
  • NVIDIA's Physical-AI push, with Inception Premier (application in flight) as the credential pathway, opens infrastructure no software company could buy on its own. The window to be a named European partner is open in 2026.
  • Operator AI fatigue. Hotel groups have invested heavily in guest-facing and revenue-management AI; almost nothing has flowed to the development side. Operators are receptive to development-side AI from owners who already speak their language.

The competitive landscape. A global scan (Grok DeepSearch + Knight Frank + Savills branded-residence reports, May 2026) finds no European Series A+ owner-developer AI product. Adjacent categories all serve the wrong customer:

  • ConTech players (Procore, Autodesk, Buildots) — general-purpose, serve the construction manager, not the developer-owner
  • Hospitality SaaS (IDeaS, Revinate, Duetto, AccorTech) — serves the operator, not the developer
  • Generative-AI consulting firms — sell hours, not product

The seat for "AI partner to luxury hospitality development" is open — and no incumbent is positioned to take it.

ClaimsMax
Adversarial AI claim defence — turning 50,000+ pages of project history into defendable positions in 30 days, not 6-12 months.
Production · Live

The pain

Global construction-dispute volumes grew approximately 163% over the past decade. In luxury hospitality, with HVS reporting the median luxury hotel development cost above $1,057,000 per key in 2025 (and many new luxury projects exceeding $2M per key), even a small percentage of contested claims translates into millions of euros of exposure on every project. The status quo defence — outside counsel + Quantity Surveyors + manual document review — runs 6 to 12 months per major claim, fees in the high six figures, and still leaves owners reactive rather than positioned.

What the product does

ClaimsMax ingests the full corpus of a development project:

  • Executed contract and addenda
  • RFIs, change orders, invoices, correspondence
  • Expert reports, monthly QS valuations, programme records

It produces audit-ready Investigation Reports mapping every contested claim line against:

  • The executed scope
  • The operator's brand-standard manual
  • Historical pricing benchmarks
  • The contract's variation mechanics

Output is litigation-grade: every flag carries a paragraph reference, a source citation, and a recommended position.

This is not pilot data: it is a continuously operating product on a real Mandarin Oriental asset against a real adversary in real insolvency proceedings.

TenderMax
The Owner's tender shield — turning 1,500-page brand standards into procurement intelligence the GC cannot pad against.
Demoable Month 6

The pain

Brand-standard manuals for ultra-luxury operators (Mandarin Oriental, Aman, Six Senses, Belmond) routinely run 1,500 to 2,000 pages. The hand-off into tender packages is currently a 12-month, consultant-heavy translation process that introduces interpretation errors, missed specifications, and procurement scope gaps the GC subsequently exploits.

Document Crunch — now Trimble-embedded since April 2026 — gives the GC an AI that flags "predatory clauses" in the Owner's tender and helps prepare delay-claim language before construction starts. The Owner currently has no AI to defend the inverse:

  • No automated check that the GC's bid actually prices the brand-standard correctly
  • No automated cross-reference against historical contractor behaviour
  • No detection of scope omissions

What the product does

TenderMax ingests four inputs:

  • The operator's brand-standard manual at full granularity
  • The architectural and MEP design packages
  • Historical contractor pricing across comparable luxury projects
  • The tender package and contractor bids as submitted

And produces three outputs:

  • Compliance map — every brand-standard requirement traced to a tender line item
  • Normalized bid comparison — each contractor's bid restated against the same scope baseline
  • Asymmetry report — every clause where the submission departs from the tender, historical pricing, or brand standard

Why it wins

The owner-side luxury-hospitality tender layer is uncontested. Three incumbents leave the slot open:

  • Trimble will not train its AI to validate tender submissions against Mandarin Oriental F&B technical specifications — TAM too narrow for a horizontal player
  • Procore is built for the GC's project manager, not the Owner's Development Director
  • Generic procurement-AI (zip-code RFP platforms, SaaS bid-management) operates at the wrong level of granularity

TenderMax aims for an internal target of 40-60% reduction in tender preparation time (from 12 months to 5-7 months) and 10-15% reduction in misalignment-driven CapEx leakage — both figures conservative against a $1M+/key luxury baseline.

OwnerView
The Owner's system of record — replacing the GC's dashboard and the Operator's quarterly reports with an independent, owner-controlled view of the asset.
Demoable Month 9

The pain

The status quo for a multi-jurisdiction luxury hotel owner is reporting they do not control:

  • The GC's Procore dashboard tells the GC's story (schedule, cost, RFIs — framed to protect GC margin)
  • The Operator's quarterly report tells the Operator's story (RevPAR, GOP, brand compliance — framed under the HMA's incentive structure)
  • Neither party is an objective system of record for the Owner

For an absentee multi-asset owner running portfolios across CH / AT / DE / FR / CY / LU, three compounding consequences:

  • Loss of grip on the asset between reporting cycles
  • No comparable cost-per-key benchmarking across currencies and construction indices
  • Quarterly surprises that compound by exit

What the product does

OwnerView consolidates four independent data streams into one owner-controlled dashboard:

  • Operator-issued reports
  • GC progress data (ingested via API from Procore / Trimble / Autodesk Construction Cloud)
  • Financing-covenant compliance status
  • Direct capex burn against budget

The product cross-normalizes cost-per-key across geographies (FX adjustment + local construction-cost-index normalization), benchmarks contractor performance across portfolio, and surfaces covenant breaches before they become disclosure events. The Brisen-internal RG7 CEO Dashboard at MO Vienna is the closest live analogue — single-asset, but proves the data architecture works. OwnerView scales this to portfolio.

SiteWatch
Owner-deployed computer-vision site supervision — replacing the contractor's weekly self-report with continuous, independent observation.
Demoable Month 12 · Inception Premier-gated

The pain

The Owner's view of weekly construction progress is the GC's self-reported PowerPoint slide. There is no independent verification of progress, quality of installation against brand standard, or safety compliance unless the Owner flies a representative to site. Buildots, OpenSpace, and Doxel exist in the market — but every one of them is GC-deployed and GC-operated. The data they capture is the GC's data, framed for the GC's GMP defence. An owner asking a GC to give them access to the GC's own Buildots dashboard for forensic audit is asking the GC to surrender its strongest negotiating asset.

What the product does

SiteWatch is owner-deployed site cameras — fixed installations at critical zones plus periodic 360° hardhat captures during Owner site visits — fed through NVIDIA's Physical-AI stack.

NVIDIA Physical-AI stack:

  • Omniverse — digital twin of the asset
  • Isaac Sim — scenario modelling
  • DGX Cloud — inference layer
  • Jetson — on-site edge processing

SiteWatch compares observed reality against three baselines:

  • The BIM model
  • The construction programme
  • The operator's brand-standard installation tolerances (marble alignment, lighting lux levels, MEP installations)

Outputs are weekly Owner-side site reports — independent of any contractor input — flagging deviations, predicting downstream rework, and surfacing safety concerns before the GC's self-report catches them.

Why it wins

The GC-side CV market is already crowded:

  • Buildots claims to catch delays "3 weeks early"
  • OpenSpace acquired Disperse in late 2025
  • Doxel deploys autonomous robots and drones

The Owner-side equivalent does not exist. SiteWatch is the suite's NVIDIA flagship — the GPU-heavy Physical-AI product that anchors the Inception Premier application and, on grant, provides the GTC-floor showcase moment. The vertical specificity (brand-standard tolerance reasoning, owner-side adversarial framing, multi-asset luxury context) is what generic ConTech CV does not deliver.

Why a suite, not four tools — the Capital and Lifecycle OS thesis

Each of the four products is independently valuable. Together they compound. Each product captures the corpus the next product depends on:

  • ClaimsMax ingests the contract / RFI / change-order corpus that TenderMax learns from to detect tender-stage asymmetries
  • TenderMax produces the scope baseline that SiteWatch compares observed reality against
  • OwnerView consolidates the financial and progress data that both ClaimsMax and SiteWatch feed
  • The loop closes when ClaimsMax — on the next dispute, the next project — leverages every prior project's litigated outcomes to defend more accurately

Four tools share data and improve each other. That is what makes this a platform, not a toolkit.

The Procore analogy is instructive but inverted. Procore became the GC's operating system by:

  • Becoming the Single Source of Truth for the jobsite
  • Monetizing the network effect of every subcontractor, architect, and inspector being forced to log in

Brisen AI inverts the target audience. Our user is not the superintendent in the mud; our user is the Chief Investment Officer in the boardroom. Our Single Source of Truth is not jobsite progress; it is the asset's full capital and lifecycle history, owned by the Owner, defended against GC and Operator counterparties. Procore is Jobsite UI. Brisen AI is Boardroom UI.
