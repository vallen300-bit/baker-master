CHAPTER 01

Executive Summary

What we are building, why it works, and what the next 12 months prove

Brisen AI is an independent product company building the European AI partner for luxury hospitality development. Anchored by Corinthia as a reference customer, credentialed by NVIDIA Inception Premier, and commercially proven by a paid Mandarin Oriental Hotel Group corporate licence extension by year-end 2026.

The opportunity. Luxury hotel development and ownership runs on spreadsheets, PDFs, and consultant memory. No European competitor at Series A or later has built an AI product suite for the developer-operator side of the industry. Brand-AI tools exist (guest experience, revenue management); development-AI tools do not. The white space is global and unclaimed.

The asset we already have. Brisen Group's Mandarin Oriental Vienna (MO Vienna) development gave us an asset most software companies never get: a production loss in a real €6M dispute, a counterparty in insolvency, and a defensible recovery built on ClaimsMax — an AI product Brisen already deployed and operationalised. ClaimsMax is live, in the wild, and demonstrably attributable to the recovery outcome.

The vertical-creation play. ClaimsMax is the production proof point. Around it we are building three more products — TenderMax, OwnerView, SiteWatch — that together cover the four hardest operational pain points in luxury hospitality development: claims defence, tender governance, ownership oversight, construction supervision. Each product addresses a multi-million-euro line item on every luxury development P&L. The suite is positioned as a single AI partnership a luxury developer-operator signs once, not four separate tools.

The proof customers. Customer #1 is Corinthia (Pisani family) — reference customer, free Y1 pilot on Corinthia Rome, paid Y2 conversion. Customer #2 is Mandarin Oriental Hotel Group corporate, structured as a licence extension off the existing MO Vienna deployment, covering MOHG-operated portfolio. Customer #2 is the kill-criterion driver — it must sign by 31 December 2026 to clear the vertical's €100K external revenue gate. Customer #3+ pipeline flows from peer luxury developer-operators reached via Corinthia / Pisani warm-introduction channel (Aman, Kempinski, Rosewood, Six Senses, Four Seasons, Belmond, One&Only).

The economics. €1.01M Year-1 base-case spend produces €180K external revenue (€100K MO Vienna → Brisen AI Holdings inter-company licence for ClaimsMax-on-Hagenauer corpus access + €80K MOHG corporate licence extension). The kill gate — >€1M spent / <€100K revenue by 2026-12-31 — clears the revenue threshold by an €80K margin but the spend threshold by only €10K. Discipline matters every line.

The capital structure. €3M outside-equity raise, two-tranche. Tranche 1: €1.5M post-money SAFE at €15M cap, closing 2026-Q2. Tranche 2: €1.5M priced Series Seed at €25-35M pre-money, closing 2026-Q4 post Customer #2 signature. Two-tranche structure preserves founder equity by pricing the second tranche on a paying-customer proof point rather than no-ARR projections.

The flip math. Probability-weighted 30-month exit ~€67M EV across five distinct pathways (hospitality-tech consolidator, ConTech consolidator, NVIDIA acqui-hire, IP-only licence, independent Series A 2027). Director net at exit, post Series A 2027 dilution and Swiss tax: ~€20-22M. This makes the vertical a real-money decision, not a side bet.

CHAPTER 02

The Company

What Brisen AI is, what makes it defensible, and why now

Brisen AI is an independent product company being spun out of Brisen Group's operating experience as a luxury hotel developer and owner. The IP holds in a dedicated entity (Brisen AI Holdings, CH Geneva) to keep the company audit-ready, separable, and acquisition-eligible from Day 1.

The owner-operator advantage. Most enterprise AI companies are sold to developers and operators by founders who have never built or run a hotel. Brisen is the inverse: we are the developer and operator, and we are building the AI we wished existed during MO Vienna. That asymmetry — owner-side product judgement, on real money, on real adversaries — is the moat. It compounds with every new MO Vienna-like episode we run through the products.

The four moats:

  • Domain depth. Director Vallen owns MO Vienna; the Brisen Group portfolio spans active assets across CH / AT / DE / FR / CY / LU. Product judgement comes from a real P&L, not from interviews.
  • Production proof. ClaimsMax is deployed against a live €6M Hagenauer claim with documented outcomes. We are not pitching from a deck; we are pitching from a case study.
  • NVIDIA credential pathway. Inception Premier (in application) opens GPU economics, GTC visibility, and Physical-AI showcase — the exact credibility infrastructure a Day-1 European startup cannot manufacture.
  • Corinthia anchor channel. Pisani family relationship is the warm-intro channel to peer luxury developer-operators no outbound sales motion can replicate.

Entity structure. Brisen AI Holdings will be incorporated in Geneva. IP — including ClaimsMax — assigns from Ellie Technologies (Philip Vallen) to Brisen AI Holdings against a 25% equity grant. Director Vallen holds 60% pre-money; Edita Vallen and family hold 15%. Inter-company mechanics route €100K Year-1 from the MO Vienna entity to Brisen AI Holdings as a clean ClaimsMax-on-Hagenauer-corpus licence fee.

CHAPTER 03

Market & Opportunity

Why this vertical, why this moment, why no one else has it

Luxury hospitality development is a multi-billion-euro global industry where every single project is hand-managed, every cost line is contested by counterparties, and every operator-developer has been failed by generic ConTech. The slot Brisen AI fills did not exist five years ago and is wide open today.

Segment. Luxury and ultra-luxury hospitality development — defined as new-build or major-refurbishment hotel projects with $50M+ capex, branded operator (or candidate for one), developer-owner ownership structure separate from operator. Europe is the launch geography. MENA, China, US follow as Corinthia and MOHG footprint pulls product into those markets.

Why now. Three forces converge in 2026:

  • Construction inflation has made claim defence, tender governance, and budget oversight existentially valuable to luxury developers. Single-digit-percentage savings on a €100M development are €5-10M absolute, line-for-line.
  • NVIDIA's Physical-AI push via Inception Premier opens credentialing infrastructure no software company could buy on its own. The window to be the named European partner is open in 2026.
  • Operator AI fatigue. Hotel groups have invested heavily in guest-facing and revenue-management AI; almost nothing has flowed to the development side. Operators are receptive to development-side AI from owners who already speak their language.

The competitive landscape. A global scan finds no European Series A+ owner-developer AI product. ConTech players (Procore, Autodesk, Buildots) are general-purpose and serve the construction manager; not the developer-owner. Hospitality SaaS (IDeaS, Revinate, Duetto, AccorTech) serves the operator; not the developer. Generative-AI consulting firms sell hours, not product. The seat for "AI partner to luxury hospitality development" is open — and no incumbent is positioned to take it.

Total addressable market. ~120 active luxury hospitality developer-operator entities globally; ~40 in Europe. Average annual development pipeline 2-5 properties each, average development capex $80-300M per property. Even a 4-product suite priced at €80-200K per developer-operator per year delivers €10-25M ARR at modest market penetration. The 30-month flip path doesn't require domination — it requires anchor + early traction.

CHAPTER 04

Product — The 4-Product Suite

Four hardest pain points in luxury hospitality development, one AI partnership

The product portfolio is built around the moments where money is most at risk in a luxury hospitality development: claim defence, tender governance, ownership reporting, on-site construction supervision. Each product is a stand-alone tool; together they form the operating system.

ClaimsMax
Live · Production
AI-powered contractor claim analysis, defence, and litigation support. Anchors the suite. Deployed against the €6M Hagenauer claim at MO Vienna with documented outcome. Already drawing external user attention (E+H legal counsel logging in to view investigation reports). Year-1 revenue: €100K MO Vienna inter-company licence + €80K MOHG corporate licence extension.
TenderMax
Demoable by Month 6
Tender analysis, bid normalization, and counterparty-pattern intelligence for development procurement. Cross-references historical contractor behaviour against current bid sheets. Designed to be the first AI artefact a developer-operator's procurement team uses before opening a tender package.
OwnerView
Demoable by Month 9
Ownership-side reporting layer that consolidates operator-issued reports, GC progress, financing covenants, and capex burn into a single live view for non-resident owners. Built for absentee owner-developers running multi-jurisdiction portfolios who cannot fly to site every week.
SiteWatch
Demoable by Month 12
Computer-vision construction supervision powered by NVIDIA Physical-AI stack. Inception Premier-gated: descoped from the Y1 suite if Premier slips past 2026-08-11 (GPU economics fail without $100K AWS credits + 30% off DGX Cloud). On-track delivery makes it the suite's flagship NVIDIA-showcase artefact.
Product Economics, Year 1

Total Y1 product build budget: €510K across the four products (ClaimsMax €130K hardening · TenderMax €170K · OwnerView €100K · SiteWatch €110K). External revenue Y1: €180K base case — all from ClaimsMax. 84% of build budget produces 0% of Y1 revenue. ClaimsMax carries the kill-gate math; TenderMax / OwnerView / SiteWatch are funded roadmap, not vapor.
