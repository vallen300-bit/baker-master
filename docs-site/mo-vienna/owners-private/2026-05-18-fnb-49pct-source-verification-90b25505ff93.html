The −49.4% number is real.

Below: a one-page walkthrough of the math and the documents that produce it.

1. Christian Gradnitzer's email of 11 May 2026 puts F&B Departmental Profit for 2026 at €274,406 against a 2026 budget of €542,096 — a miss of 49.4%.

All three figures — €274,406, €542,096, and the 49.4% variance — appear in the PDF Christian attached to his 11 May 2026 email. They sit on a single line of a single table.

Source of the three figures Email: Christian Gradnitzer to Brisen, 11 May 2026.
Attachment: “Mandarin Oriental, Vienna — RB&E — Positioning & Planned Changes” (one-pager PDF).
Location in PDF: page 1, financial table at the bottom of the page, row labelled “Departmental Profit”, final three columns: Fcast 2026 Total · 2026 Budget Total · Variance +(−)%.
Values read directly from those three columns: 274,406 · 542,096 · (49.4).
Where the €542,096 budget figure is anchored The formal 2026 Operating Budget for the hotel is the document “MOVIE BUDGET P&L 2026.pdf” (Brisen project folder). The F&B Departmental Contribution line appears in:
  • Schedule 2.2 (Annual P&L Summary), page 1 — Food & Beverage section, “Departmental Contribution” row, showing €615,491 for the full year.
  • Schedule 2.4 (Monthly P&L), page 2 — same row broken out month by month (Jan through Dec 2026), summing to the same €615,491.
Christian's table cites €542,096 as the “2026 Budget Total” — this differs from the Operating Budget by €73,395, because Christian groups some F&B Other Income items separately rather than inside F&B Revenue. Both numbers are in MOHG's own documents. Measured against either one, the forecast is materially below: 49.4% below Christian's own cited budget, or 55.4% below the formal Operating Budget. The attached Excel shows all three sets of numbers (budget, Q1 actuals, Christian's full-year forecast) side by side, month by month.
