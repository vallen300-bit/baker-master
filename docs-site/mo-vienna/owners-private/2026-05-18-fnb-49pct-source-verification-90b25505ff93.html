The "−49.4%" number is MOHG's, not ours

A one-page walk through the math and the document that produced it.

1. What we said in the Executive Summary

Christian Gradnitzer's 11 May one-pager forecasts FY26 F&B Departmental Profit of €274,406 against a 2026 budget of €542,096. That is a miss of −49.4% vs the budget MOHG themselves prepared, signed, and delivered to us.

Source: MOHG "Mandarin Oriental, Vienna · RB&E — Positioning & Planned Changes", one-pager dated 11 May 2026, page 1, bottom financial table. Local file: 1_ACTIVE_PROJECTS/01_MOVIE_PROJECT/01_MOHG/2026-05-11-christian-gradnitzer-atelier7-onepager.pdf. Intake channel: Balazs Csepregi (Brisen Projects & Transactions).

2. The table — copied verbatim from MOHG's document

The number comes from a single line in a table MOHG themselves built and sent. Below is exactly what appears in the bottom three columns of the one-pager's financial table, on the row labelled "Departmental Profit" (the bottom row of that table).
Row from MOHG's tableFcast 2026 Total2026 Budget TotalVariance +(-)%
Departmental Profit (F&B)274,406542,096(49.4)

Important. The "(49.4)" figure in the rightmost column is MOHG's own calculation of the variance. We did not compute that number — MOHG did. We are simply reading the column they printed.

3. The arithmetic, step by step

  1. €274,406 ÷ €542,096 = 0.5062 → Christian's forecast = 50.6% of the budget MOHG built.
  2. 1 − 0.5062 = 0.4938 → forecast is 49.4% below MOHG's own budget.
  3. This matches the "(49.4)" figure printed in the rightmost column of MOHG's own table.

7. Conclusion

The "−49.4%" figure is not an Owner-side or MOVIE Desk calculation. It is MOHG's own number, printed by MOHG in the right-most column of the same one-pager Christian sent. The arithmetic checks out (€274,406 ÷ €542,096 = 50.6% of budget = 49.4% miss). The Q1 actuals embedded in the same table tie exactly to the −€612K F&B Departmental Contribution figure we cite from the Monthly Statements. It is the operator's own forecast vs the operator's own budget, on the same row of the same MOHG document.
