## 9. GRUENTEN BERGWELT -- FAILED EUR 30M TOURISM PROJECT

This is a significant additional data point on Martin Hagenauer's judgment and risk appetite.

**The Plan:** The Hagenauer family's BergWelt GmbH purchased the Gruentenlifte (ski lifts) at the Gruenten mountain near Rettenberg and proposed a EUR 30 million investment to build:
- A modern 10-person cable car (2,300m span)
- A detachable 6-person chairlift
- New gastronomy facilities (Gruentenhuette)
- Restoration of hiking trails

**The Outcome: PROJECT FAILED.**
- Triggered intense local opposition and environmental protests
- Citizens' initiative "Rettet den Gruenten" organized resistance
- BUND Naturschutz (environmental group) opposed the plans
- The Alpgenossenschaft Gruenten (local alpine cooperative) refused to register the necessary land mortgage
- Rettenberg Mayor Nikolaus Weissinger announced the family had abandoned the project

**Significance for Negotiation:**
- Demonstrates a pattern of **overambitious capital allocation** -- attempting a EUR 30M tourism project while simultaneously running EUR 140M+ construction projects
- Shows Martin Hagenauer's willingness to take large risks in sectors outside his core competence
- The project failed due to community opposition, suggesting he underestimated stakeholder management
- Capital tied up in this failed venture may have contributed to the liquidity crisis
