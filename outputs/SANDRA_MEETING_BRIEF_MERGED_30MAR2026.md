## LINE-BY-LINE REBUTTAL (8 Claims)

### Claim 1: "Brisen Development GmbH largely stopped making payments on outstanding interim invoices as of January 2026"

**Rebuttal:**
- Brisen paid EUR 130.5M of approximately EUR 142M contract value -- over 95%.
- Payments were made against verified work completions throughout the entire contract period.
- What stopped in early 2026 was Brisen's willingness to pay DISPUTED invoices for which Hagenauer had not provided adequate documentation, and which included charges for work not completed to specification.
- Hagenauer never submitted a Schlussrechnung (final invoice) despite repeated written demands from Brisen.
- You cannot claim "non-payment" when you haven't presented a proper bill.

**Source:** E+H correspondence; payment records; Schlussrechnung demands.

### Claim 2: "Outstanding claims of approximately EUR 21 million"

**Rebuttal:**
- This is Hagenauer's gross claim, unilaterally stated, unverified.
- Hagenauer's own Section 1170b security demand started at EUR 13.2M and was self-reduced to EUR 5.85M -- a more than 50% reduction that the article conveniently omits.
- Brisen has documented counterclaims of EUR 5.2M+ for construction defects, delays, and completion costs.
- Brisen identified EUR 7.5M in disputed charges.
- Net position after counterclaims is dramatically lower than EUR 21M.
- A company winding down voluntarily (their own filing says "continuation not planned") does not behave like one owed EUR 21M.

**Source:** E+H legal analysis; Section 1170b correspondence; Brisen damage documentation.

### Claim 3: "Blocked payments through tactical complaints regarding defects and announcement of extensive remedial work -- most recently approximately EUR 7.4 million"

**Rebuttal:**
- The EUR 7.4M represents legitimate, documented construction defects -- not "tactical complaints."
- These defects were identified by independent experts and documented according to OENORM standards.
- Calling contractual defect claims "tactical" is Hagenauer's litigation framing, not a factual description.
- A EUR 250M luxury hotel project with 2,000+ documented defects has every right to withhold payment until defects are rectified. This is standard Austrian construction law (Section 1170 ABGB / OENORM B 2110).

**Source:** Defect documentation; expert reports; Brisen letter of 13 March 2026.

### Claim 4: "Significant delays" caused by heritage preservation, subway tunnel, "massive project changes initiated by the client" and "over 100 project amendment notifications"

**Rebuttal:**
- Heritage preservation and subway requirements were known to Hagenauer from the start and priced into the contract.
- Brisen's position: the change orders were overwhelmingly initiated BY Hagenauer, not by the client -- resulting from Hagenauer's own planning errors, coordination failures, and scope mismanagement.
- Christine Saehn herself admitted "Hagenauer took on too many projects simultaneously" -- the delays were a resource problem, not a client problem.
- The hotel opened on schedule in December 2025. If "massive client changes" had actually occurred, the hotel would not be operational.

**Source:** Project records; Saehn admission; E+H analysis.

### Claim 5: Hagenauer demanded EUR 5.85M security under Section 1170b -- "rejected by Brisen"

**Rebuttal:**
- The original demand was EUR 13.2M. It was self-reduced to EUR 5.85M after Brisen's lawyers demonstrated the calculations were wrong.
- A more than 50% self-reduction in a security demand is a devastating admission that the original claim was inflated.
- Brisen disputed the demand on substantive legal grounds -- this is not "rejection" but a legitimate contractual dispute.
- The demand is now moot: the insolvency administrator takes over all claims.

**Source:** Section 1170b correspondence (Buhl letter 17 Feb; E+H response).

### Claim 6: "Client's representative failed to fulfill coordination obligations to Mandarin Oriental" and obligations for permits

**Rebuttal:**
- Coordination with Mandarin Oriental Hotel Group is a matter between the hotel operator and the development company -- not the general contractor's responsibility or concern.
- Building permits were obtained and the hotel is open and operational. The permit claim is factually refuted by the building's existence.
- This framing attempts to shift blame for Hagenauer's own coordination failures onto the client.

**Source:** Building permits; MO operational status.

### Claim 7: "Project volume approximately EUR 150 million"

**Rebuttal:**
- The total project investment exceeds EUR 250 million. The EUR 150M figure represents only the GU contract value.
- By citing the lower number, the article makes the EUR 21M disputed amount appear proportionally larger (14% vs 8.4%).
- This is a framing choice, not a factual error -- but it distorts the scale of the project.

### Claim 8: Naming Dimitry and Edita Vallen as "exclusively authorized to issue and receive binding declarations"

**Rebuttal:**
- This is a selective quote from one clause of the GU contract (Punkt 26 TUV), taken out of context.
- Brisen Development GmbH has professional managing directors (Thomas Leitner, formerly also Conrad Bauer) registered in the Austrian commercial register (Firmenbuch FN 444495 w).
- Dimitry acted on the basis of a Power of Attorney issued by Brisen Dev -- as a shareholder representative, not as a managing director.
- Thomas and Conrad, as registered MDs of Brisen Dev, signed the 2023 contract amendment -- demonstrating the MDs exercised real authority.
- The OGH four-element test for faktischer Geschaeftsfuehrer requires cumulative proof that fewer than 5% of Austrian cases achieve.
- E+H categorically rejected this characterization on 22 March 2026.

**Source:** Firmenbuch; E+H legal opinion (28 March); PoA documentation; 2023 amendment signatures.
