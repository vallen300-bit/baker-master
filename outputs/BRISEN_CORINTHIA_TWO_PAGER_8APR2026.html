The Owner's Pain
EUR 100-300M commitment. 3-5 year timeline. The owner bears 100% of the capital risk — yet sits farthest from the truth.

Development Phase

  • Design inefficiencies and procurement errors. Brand standards translated into specifications by hand. Errors compound across concurrent projects. How many design decisions are repeated without benchmarking against past outcomes?
  • No independent view of progress. Reports come from the party with the strongest incentive to overstate completion. No real-time verification.
  • Delays and cost overruns. Scope changes cascade through the programme unchecked. No system connects a design change to the budget, the timeline, and the financing covenant — simultaneously.

Operations & Oversight

  • Information asymmetry. Operators obscure operational weaknesses. Brands limit data access under system sovereignty. The owner who carries the risk has the least visibility.
  • No early warning. Critical deviations surface in monthly reports — six weeks late. By the time the owner sees the problem, the downward spiral has begun.
  • Possible claims left on the table. How many recoverable items go unnoticed in documentation no team has time to fully review?
  • Decades of precious development experience. How much of it is in a structured database? How many decisions from project 5 informed project 15?
Context: HospitalityInside (Jan 2026) describes hotel insolvencies as "the collective failure of an ecosystem" built on information asymmetry and zero data-driven controls.

A Market Nobody Serves
To our knowledge, no product exists for the luxury hotel owner-developer. Construction tech serves contractors. Revenue AI serves operators. The owner is alone.

Why the gap persists

  • Data trapped in individual projects. Unstructured, dispersed across departments, never digitised. A group with 17 hotels starts project 18 without the structured benefit of the first 17.
  • Domain too complex for generic tools. A dozen disciplines in parallel — architecture, heritage regulation, brand standards, FF&E, construction, residences, pre-opening. No platform reasons across all of them.
  • Requires both hotel development experience and AI capability. That combination has not existed until now.

ClaimsMax — what it does
