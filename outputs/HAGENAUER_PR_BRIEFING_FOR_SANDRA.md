## 4. THE DISPUTE — WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENED

### 4.1 The Contract

Brisen engaged Hagenauer under a **maximum price guarantee (GMP) contract** — a standard form in large-scale Austrian construction. Under a GMP contract, the contractor bears the risk of cost overruns. The contractor's total remuneration is capped at the agreed maximum price.

### 4.2 Payment History

**Brisen has paid Hagenauer approximately EUR 130 million**, representing 95-96% of the total contract value. At no point has Brisen refused to pay for properly rendered and accepted works. Every payment made by Brisen was in accordance with the contractual payment schedule against verified work completions.

### 4.3 The Missing Final Invoice

Despite numerous written demands from Brisen, Hagenauer has **not submitted a final invoice (Schlussrechnung)**. Hagenauer's stated reason: "for legal reasons, he cannot provide a final invoice."

This is a critical fact. Under Austrian construction law, the contractor is obligated to submit a final accounting. Without a Schlussrechnung, the precise outstanding amount cannot be determined. It is contradictory to claim "non-payment" while simultaneously refusing to submit the invoice that would establish what is owed.

### 4.4 The EUR 15 Million Claim — And Its Collapse

In approximately February 2026, Hagenauer demanded a security guarantee of approximately EUR 15 million under Section 1170b of the Austrian Civil Code (ABGB). This provision allows a contractor to demand security for outstanding claims.

**What happened next is telling:** After Brisen's legal team (E+H Rechtsanwaelte) presented its calculations — documenting counterclaims for defects, delays, and contractor mismanagement — Hagenauer **reduced his own demand from EUR 15 million to EUR 5 million within three months**. A two-thirds reduction in the claimed amount.

This reduction proves that the original EUR 15 million figure was **deliberately inflated**. It was a pressure tactic, not a reflection of genuine amounts owed.

### 4.5 Brisen's Counterclaims

Brisen has substantive, documented counterclaims against Hagenauer including:

- **Construction defects** requiring rectification
- **Delays** attributable to Hagenauer's project management failures
- **Mismanagement of the construction site**, resulting in cost overruns that Hagenauer is now attempting to pass on to Brisen
- **Subcontractor claims** arising from Hagenauer's failure to properly manage his supply chain

These counterclaims are being pursued through proper legal channels by E+H Rechtsanwaelte, one of Austria's leading commercial law firms.

### 4.6 The Contract Termination

On approximately 14-15 March 2026, Hagenauer terminated the TC contract, citing the expiry of the contractual deadline for provision of security. This termination is contested by Brisen on substantive legal grounds — the security demand itself was inflated and is subject to legal dispute.

### 4.7 Approved Language

> "This is a contractual dispute between a project developer and a construction contractor — a situation that occurs regularly in large-scale construction projects. Brisen Development has met its contractual payment obligations, having paid over 95% of the contract value. The remaining amounts are subject to legitimate counterclaims for documented defects and delays. The matter is being resolved through proper legal channels."

## 14. APPENDIX: KEY AUSTRIAN LEGAL CONCEPTS (for Sandra's Reference)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Section 1170b ABGB | Austrian Civil Code provision allowing a contractor to demand security for outstanding claims. If security is not provided within the statutory period, the contractor gains the right to terminate. |
| Schlussrechnung | Final invoice / final accounting. The contractor's obligation to submit a complete accounting of all works performed and amounts claimed. Without it, the final balance cannot be determined. |
| Totalunternehmer (TU) | Total contractor / general contractor responsible for delivering the complete construction works. |
| GMP (Garantierter Maximalpreis) | Maximum price guarantee contract. The contractor bears cost overrun risk above the agreed cap. |
| Geschaeftsfuehrer | Managing director of a GmbH (limited liability company). Registered in the Firmenbuch (commercial register). |
| Firmenbuch | Austrian commercial register — publicly accessible record of company details including managing directors. |
| Baustellenstoerung | Site disturbance — legal concept where a contractor's mismanagement causes disruption and additional costs to subcontractors. |
| Ersatzvornahme | Substitute performance — the principal's right to engage third parties to complete or rectify works at the contractor's expense. |
