NVIDIA Give/Get Precedents — Director Report

Modelling what NVIDIA gives vs gets, for the NVIDIA × Mandarin Oriental × Brisen “AI lighthouse hotel” pitch (Santa Clara, to Pradeep Storer). Five verified precedents + synthesis. · Researcher, 15 June 2026 · bus #3041
The smallest ask is the proven one NVIDIA gives software, solution-engineers, co-marketing and named design-partner status; it gets a flagship reference deployment plus GPU pull-through. A named, co-branded “lighthouse” customer is exactly the shape NVIDIA has repeatedly said yes to. Lead there — not with a capital ask.

Headline

Across automotive, enterprise software, retail and infrastructure, NVIDIA’s give/get is consistent: it contributes platform/software access, solution-architect time, co-marketing/PR and design-partner status, and takes back a flagship reference deployment, vertical validation and GPU/CUDA pull-through. Cash and free GPUs are rare at the customer tier. NVIDIA does deploy capital aggressively — but almost only where the partner buys GPUs at scale (“circular financing”). A hotel is not that profile, so the realistic lever for MO is the lighthouse, not equity.

Does NVIDIA put in capital? Yes — but only at GPU scale

NVIDIA disclosed a ~$17.5B private-company / infrastructure portfolio by early 2025, scaling fast through 2025–26. Every large check tracks one variable: how many GPUs the partner buys or deploys.

The through-line: NVIDIA finances demand for its own chips. A hotel that consumes modest compute is off-pattern for an equity ask — treat any NVIDIA capital as upside only, never the opening.

What we should steal

  • Frame MO as NVIDIA’s first luxury-hospitality lighthouse — the smallest ask NVIDIA reliably grants; bring an integrator so NVIDIA only supplies software + solution-architects + PR.
  • Offer the venue’s data / 3D twin + a public reference back — that is the “get” NVIDIA values most (the Lowe’s pattern).
  • Tie NVIDIA’s upside to recurring guest-tech revenue, not a one-off (the Mercedes-Benz model) — gives NVIDIA a long-horizon reason to invest engineering.

What we should not do

  • Don’t lead with an equity / capital ask — off-pattern for a non-GPU-scale customer; signals we misread NVIDIA’s model.
  • Don’t expect free GPUs or dedicated allocation in the opening — not disclosed in any customer-tier precedent.
  • Don’t overpromise GPU pull-through we can’t deliver — inflated compute claims get discounted.

Open questions for the pitch team

Name an integrator partner? An Accenture/Deloitte-type in the pitch would mirror the Lighthouse three-way structure and lower NVIDIA’s lift. Recommendation: yes.

Is MO willing to be a public co-branded reference + contribute property data / 3D-twin content? That is the core “get”; without it the ask weakens.

Floor vs stretch ask. Floor = software + solution-architects + joint PR; stretch = GPU credits / priority allocation. Capital is not realistic — confirm we exclude it.
