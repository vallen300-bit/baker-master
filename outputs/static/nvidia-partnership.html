IV

The Origin Story

How a construction crisis created an AI company

The Project

European hotel developers. 35 years, over a billion in projects. Mandarin Oriental Vienna -- EUR 250 million, opened December 2025. Ultra-luxury, heritage building, the most complex type of hotel development.

The Crisis

The general contractor collapsed mid-project. Half a million documents. EUR 15 million in disputed claims. Consultants quoted 18 months to resolve. The project could not wait 18 months.

The Build

Built an agentic AI system out of necessity -- not as a product company, but because there was no other way. The system reads contracts, cross-references invoices, maps every contractor obligation against every deliverable.

The Result

EUR 6 million in recoverable damages identified in weeks -- not months. What would have taken a team of lawyers and claims consultants over a year was accomplished by AI in a fraction of the time.

The Validation

"Ten companies would buy this tomorrow."
-- 60-year-old construction expert, after initially calling it nonsense

The Insight

Hospitality is the finished product. But to get there, you go through land, financing, construction, fit-out, claims, operations, asset management. Every stage is data-heavy, near-zero AI adoption -- and the same person pays for all of it: the developer-owner. That is the underserved buyer. Not the chains.
