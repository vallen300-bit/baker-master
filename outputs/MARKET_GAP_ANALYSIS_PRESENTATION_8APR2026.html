Q4
Why has nobody filled this gap?
Five structural reasons why an owner-developer cannot simply use Procore + Autodesk + a consulting firm.
Finding: The gap is structural, not accidental
1
Financial-to-Physical Loop
The owner needs to see how a scope change ripples through total cost, financing covenants, and projected IRR — simultaneously. Procore tracks what was built. It cannot model what should be built for optimal returns.
"If I upgrade the spa wing by EUR 3M, what happens to my loan covenant, my IRR, and my break-even occupancy rate?" — No generic tool can answer this.
2
Multi-Jurisdictional Regulatory Complexity
Luxury hotels in Europe face heritage preservation, zoning, environmental assessments, and multi-stage permitting. Generic tools handle building codes. They cannot navigate the politically charged, evolving regulatory landscape of a specific European city.
Building a luxury hotel in Rome vs. Dubai vs. Maldives — three entirely different regulatory universes. No generic tool contains this knowledge.
3
Value Engineering Through the Luxury Lens
The owner doesn't want the cheapest solution — they want the optimal value solution that maintains the luxury guest experience. This requires understanding material aesthetics, brand standards, sustainability ratings, and cost simultaneously.
"Does this sustainable facade solution meet the brand's required level of opulence?" — A question no generic ConTech tool can even parse.
4
Stakeholder Coordination
The owner manages lender expectations, brand requirements, local government relations, architect vision, and contractor delivery — simultaneously. Generic tools treat all stakeholders equally. The owner needs conflict detection and relationship-aware intelligence.
When the lender's cost requirement conflicts with the brand's amenity standard — who arbitrates? The owner. With no AI support.
5
Predictive Pre-Operational Modeling
Development decisions must be informed by future operating performance. No ConTech tool connects the build to the business case.
"If I add 3 premium meeting rooms, how does my projected occupancy change in years 1-3, and does that justify the additional EUR 2M in construction cost?"
Q5
The Definitive Market Positioning
For Corinthia Group board and QP leadership.

Artificial Intelligence is not a novelty; it is a rapidly maturing frontier. Sophisticated tools already exist in adjacent spaces — construction management platforms for general contractors, revenue optimisation systems for hotel operators. These tools validate the potential of AI. However, they solve linear, operational challenges. They are fundamentally incapable of managing the multidimensional challenge of creation.

The structural gap lies not in AI's capability, but in its application. No system exists to holistically fuse the complex confluence of financial viability, regulatory compliance, brand luxury positioning, and projected owner's return into a single, actionable intelligence model. This is the owner-developer's core concern: structuring a project that is not merely built, but is financially guaranteed to succeed at the highest level of luxury service for decades.

This gap has persisted because development knowledge is locked inside family-owned groups — proprietary data that cannot be scraped, bought, or reverse-engineered. The domain is too complex for generic tools. And the decision-makers who allocate EUR 100-300M to a single project will only trust technology built by people who have built what they are building.

This is why the combination of Corinthia's unparalleled development depth — 60 years, 17+ properties, the full vertical through QP and Disigneq — coupled with Brisen's AI capability proven on a EUR 250M Mandarin Oriental project, is not merely advantageous. It is structurally necessary. No other combination possesses both the proprietary data and the deployed AI products required to fill this gap.

Honest Caveats

What we acknowledge internally — intellectual honesty strengthens the argument.

Transparency Notes

  • Generic tools ARE used on hotel projects — Procore, Autodesk, etc. are used by contractors building hotels. But they serve the contractor's workflow, not the owner's investment thesis.
  • Big Four consultancies offer AI-assisted services — but as bespoke consulting engagements at EUR 500K-2M per engagement. Not productised. Not scalable. Not available to independent owner-developers.
  • In-house tools likely exist — large institutional developers (Brookfield, Related, Hines) may have internal tools. But these are proprietary, not productised, and not hospitality-specific.
  • The claim is strongest when framed precisely: "No AI product exists for the luxury hotel owner-developer" — not "No AI is used in hotel construction." Precision matters.
