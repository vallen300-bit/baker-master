## 2. THE "FAKTISCHER GESCHAEFTSFUEHRER" THREAT -- LEGAL ANALYSIS

### 2.1 What Nemetschke Is Arguing

Alfred Nemetschke (NHK Rechtsanwalte) has constructed a deliberate legal narrative over ten days (18-27 March 2026) designed to achieve the following:

**The claim:** Dimitry Vallen and Edita Vallen are not merely shareholders or indirect beneficial owners of Brisen Development GmbH. They are, Nemetschke argues, its *faktische Geschaeftsfuehrer* -- persons who, without formal appointment, exercised actual comprehensive direction of the company, displacing the formally appointed Geschaeftsfuehrer (Thomas Leitner, and previously also Thomas Weiss).

**The legal consequence sought:** Durchgriffshaftung (veil piercing) and/or personal liability under:
- **Section 25 GmbHG** (liability of managing directors for breach of duty)
- **Section 69 IO** (duty to file for insolvency without culpable delay -- Insolvenzverschleppung)
- **Existenzvernichtungshaftung** (liability for destruction of the company's existence, per German BGH II ZR 300/02, increasingly referenced by Austrian courts)
- **General tortious liability under Section 1295 ABGB** (if the de facto directors' conduct caused damage to Hagenauer)

If successful, the Vallens would be personally liable for Hagenauer's claims -- not limited to the corporate assets of Brisen Development GmbH.

### 2.2 Why They Are Doing It

The "faktischer Geschaeftsfuehrer" theory serves four distinct strategic purposes for the Hagenauer side:

**(a) Deflection of Martin Hagenauer's own Insolvenzverschleppung liability.** Under Section 69(2) IO, the managing directors of an insolvent company bear personal liability if they failed to file for insolvency without culpable delay (schuldhafte Verzoegerung). The insolvency administrator will investigate whether Martin Hagenauer (sole MD since Buhl's departure on 24 February) filed too late. By attributing the insolvency to the Vallens' conduct -- specifically, the refusal to provide Section 1170b security -- Hagenauer creates a narrative defense: "I did not cause the insolvency; they did, by strangling our cash flow."

**(b) Creating a personal claim for the insolvency administrator.** An insolvency administrator (Insolvenzverwalter) is obligated to pursue all available claims to maximize the estate. If the Vallens can be characterized as faktische Geschaeftsfuehrer, the administrator has a potential personal claim against them -- a claim that exists independently of the corporate-to-corporate relationship between Brisen Development and Hagenauer Austria. This transforms a contractor-developer dispute into a personal liability matter with potentially unlimited exposure.

**(c) Redirecting subcontractors toward Brisen.** By naming the Vallens in a letter to all subcontractors, Saehn/Nemetschke are doing two things: (i) providing subcontractors with an alternative target for their unpaid claims (the Vallens personally, rather than the now-insolvent Hagenauer), and (ii) poisoning Brisen's ability to contract directly with those same subcontractors for completion works. If subcontractors believe the Vallens are personally responsible for non-payment, they will demand onerous terms or refuse to work with Brisen at all.

**(d) Nemetschke's fee strategy and reputation play.** A faktischer Geschaeftsfuehrer claim against wealthy individuals is a more valuable mandate than a standard corporate insolvency claim. It also generates media interest (see the ImmoFokus articles) which serves Nemetschke's professional reputation as an aggressive litigator.

### 2.3 The Austrian Law Test: OGH Jurisprudence

Austrian courts have developed a four-element cumulative test for faktischer Geschaeftsfuehrer status. All four must be met:

| Element | Test | Bar |
|---------|------|-----|
| **1. Continuous and comprehensive direction** | The person must exercise ongoing, not occasional, management of the company's affairs. Isolated involvement in individual transactions does not suffice. | HIGH -- occasional participation in negotiations is insufficient |
| **2. Displacement of the formal Geschaeftsfuehrer** | The formal GF must be reduced to a puppet (Strohmann). The de facto director must have effectively replaced the GF's decision-making authority. | VERY HIGH -- requires evidence that the formal GF had no genuine autonomy |
| **3. External appearance** | Third parties must have perceived the person as the actual decision-maker. This is typically evidenced by who signed contracts, who corresponded with counterparties, who attended critical meetings. | MEDIUM -- depends on documentary evidence |
| **4. The formal GF is a puppet** | Closely related to element 2, but focused on the subjective position of the formal GF: did they understand themselves to be acting on instructions, or did they exercise genuine judgment? | HIGH -- testimony of the formal GF is critical |

**OGH precedent:** Austrian courts pierce the corporate veil in fewer than 5% of cases where it is attempted. The Oberster Gerichtshof (Supreme Court) has consistently held that shareholder oversight, even active shareholder oversight, does not equate to de facto directorship. The test requires *displacement*, not *involvement*.

### 2.4 Application to This Case

**Our strongest arguments:**

**(i) Thomas Leitner exercises genuine authority.** Leitner is the formally appointed Geschaeftsfuehrer of Brisen Development GmbH. The documentary record demonstrates his independent exercise of authority:
- He drafted and sent the Schadenersatz letter (3 March)
- He coordinated with E+H on legal strategy independently
- He managed the press response to ImmoFokus (sent response to Charles Steiner on 16 March)
- He is the designated spokesperson for all media inquiries
- He corresponds directly with Blaschka and Ofenheimer on the subcontractor letter, torpedo action, and Aukera summary
- He manages the site team (Vladimir Moravcik, Thomas Bauer) without Dimitry's involvement in operational decisions

**(ii) Shareholder participation in significant negotiations is legitimate.** The Feb 7 meeting (97 minutes) is the strongest piece of evidence Nemetschke could point to -- Dimitry and Edita participated directly in negotiations with Christine Saehn. However:
- Section 20 GmbHG explicitly preserves the Gesellschafterversammlung's right to give instructions to the Geschaeftsfuehrer
- A shareholder attending a major commercial negotiation alongside the GF is fundamentally different from a shareholder *replacing* the GF
- The meeting transcript shows Dimitry was one of several participants, not the sole decision-maker
- The action item from the meeting explicitly called for "senior decision-makers (e.g., Martin)" -- treating both sides' principals as parallel, not as the Vallens being "in charge" of Hagenauer

**(iii) Edita's involvement is that of an active shareholder, not a GF.** Edita's documented activities include:
- Financial analysis and investigation reports (ClaimsMax)
- Drafting the subcontractor letter (which was then reviewed by Thomas Leitner, Thomas Bauer, and E+H -- demonstrating a collaborative process, not unilateral direction)
- Participating in PR strategy discussions
- Signing the Polish torpedo power of attorney (as an authorized signatory, not as GF)

None of these activities rise to the level of *displacing* the formal GF. They are consistent with an engaged, capable shareholder performing oversight functions -- which is both lawful and commonplace in Austrian GmbH practice.

**(iv) The Firmenbuch is conclusive on formal appointment.** The Austrian commercial register (Firmenbuch) shows Thomas Leitner as Geschaeftsfuehrer. Neither Dimitry nor Edita Vallen has ever been entered in the Firmenbuch as a director or authorized signatory (Prokurist) of Brisen Development GmbH.

### 2.5 The TUV Punkt 26 Question -- CRITICAL GAP

Nemetschke's confidence in citing a specific contract clause is concerning. In his 22 March email, he wrote with obvious conviction: *"Sie wollen doch bitte Punkt 26. des TUV nachlesen"* -- "please read Clause 26 of the TUV."

**What we do not know:** The exact text of TUV Clause 26. This is the single most important unknown in our defense.

**What we suspect:** Based on Charles Steiner's media inquiry (16 March), which asked: "The article also states that in the general contractor agreement with Brisen Development, you and your wife are listed as the sole authorized representatives for the Mandarin Oriental project" -- Punkt 26 may contain a representation clause naming Dimitry and/or Edita Vallen as "authorized representatives" (Ansprechpartner, Bevollmaechtigte, or similar) for the project.

**Why this matters:** If the TUV explicitly names the Vallens as the persons authorized to make decisions on behalf of Brisen Development, Nemetschke will argue this is contractual evidence of their de facto directorship. This does not automatically satisfy the OGH four-element test -- being named as an authorized contact is fundamentally different from being a de facto director -- but it is a vulnerability that must be understood, briefed, and prepared for.

**RECOMMENDATION FOR E+H:** Punkt 26 of the TUV must be reviewed and briefed as Priority 1. If it contains language naming the Vallens as authorized representatives, the defense must address why such a designation is standard commercial practice (many contracts name the UBO as a project contact) and does not satisfy the displacement-of-GF requirement.

### 2.6 The Section 1170b Connection

Nemetschke is attempting to construct a chain of causation:

1. The Vallens (as faktische Geschaeftsfuehrer) decided not to provide the Section 1170b security
2. This decision caused Hagenauer to terminate the TUV
3. The termination caused the insolvency
4. Therefore the Vallens personally caused the insolvency

**This chain fails on multiple levels:**

**(a) The Section 1170b obligation runs between contractual parties.** The security obligation under Section 1170b ABGB attaches to the *Werkbesteller* (client) -- that is, Brisen Development GmbH. It is a corporate obligation. Personal involvement in a GmbH's commercial decision does not create personal liability. If it did, every shareholder who voted against a payment would be personally liable -- an absurd result that Austrian law does not support.

**(b) The decision not to provide security was legally advised.** Ofenheimer's 23 February analysis concluded that Hagenauer's claim did not meet the three cumulative conditions for a valid Section 1170b demand. The decision was based on genuine legal grounds (Brisen's counterclaims exceeded the demanded security), not on bad faith or intent to harm.

**(c) Hagenauer's insolvency was not caused by Brisen's conduct.** The evidence shows:
- Hagenauer was simultaneously managing EUR 140M+ in Hamburg (Campus Schluetersstrasse) and EUR 140M in Vienna -- EUR 280M+ on a EUR 100M annual revenue base
- Multiple subcontractors went insolvent under Hagenauer's management *before* any payment dispute with Brisen
- Brisen paid 95-96% of the contract value (EUR 130.5M of ~EUR 140M)
- Christine Saehn herself admitted in the Feb 7 meeting: "Hagenauer took on too many projects simultaneously"
- Dominik Buhl (the commercial/strategic co-MD) departed 24 February -- three weeks before insolvency
- The German Schutzschirmverfahren relates to the Hamburg project, not Vienna

## 3. THE SCHLUSSRECHNUNG QUESTION

A fundamental question hangs over this dispute: **Why did Hagenauer never submit a Schlussrechnung (final invoice)?**

In the Feb 7 meeting, both sides acknowledged the need for a comprehensive reconciliation. Brisen formally demanded the Schlussrechnung. Hagenauer's response, relayed through correspondence: "for legal reasons he cannot provide a final invoice."

This is extraordinary. The Schlussrechnung is the standard mechanism for resolving final account disputes in Austrian construction law. Hagenauer's refusal to submit one is, in our assessment, deliberate and strategic. Here is why:

### Theory 1: The Numbers Would Not Survive Scrutiny

Hagenauer's initial claim was EUR 13.2M. Within weeks, this was **self-reduced to EUR 5.85M** -- a two-thirds reduction. This self-reduction destroys the quantum credibility of the original claim. If Hagenauer submitted a formal Schlussrechnung, it would have to commit to specific numbers that could then be subjected to expert verification (Sachverstaendiger). Hagenauer's own internal calculations apparently do not support EUR 13.2M, and possibly do not even support EUR 5.85M.

### Theory 2: The Net Position Probably Favors Brisen

Brisen's internal analysis (Feb 25 meeting): on an actual cost basis, approximately EUR 4.85M is genuinely outstanding, against EUR 5.2M in documented damage claims. **Net: Hagenauer owes Brisen.** If a formal Schlussrechnung were submitted and subjected to expert review, this net position would become documented and enforceable -- the opposite of what Hagenauer wants.

### Theory 3: The Schlussrechnung Triggers the Sachverstaendiger

Under Austrian construction practice, once a Schlussrechnung is submitted, the counterparty is entitled to engage an independent expert (Sachverstaendiger) to verify the claimed amounts. Per the Feb 25 meeting transcript, Hagenauer specifically refused this mechanism. They did not want an independent expert reviewing their numbers. This strongly suggests the numbers are indefensible.

### Theory 4: Section 1170b Was the Real Strategy

The sequence is telling: rather than submitting a Schlussrechnung and pursuing commercial resolution, Hagenauer's approach was to (a) demand EUR 13.2M in security under Section 1170b, (b) terminate the contract when security was refused, and (c) pursue claims through litigation and media pressure. The Section 1170b demand was not a genuine attempt at payment reconciliation -- it was a mechanism to create a termination right while preserving an inflated and unscrutinized claim.

### Theory 5: Insolvency Was Already Being Planned

Dominik Buhl departed on 24 February. The German Schutzschirmverfahren was filed on 16 March -- three weeks later. It is virtually certain that the insolvency trajectory was known within Hagenauer management well before the Section 1170b letter of 17 February. In an insolvency context, an ambiguous, unsubstantiated claim of EUR 13.2M has more leverage than a documented Schlussrechnung that might show a net zero or net negative position. The insolvency administrator can present the EUR 13.2M figure to creditors as a potential recovery asset; a Schlussrechnung showing EUR 5.85M (or less) would be far less useful.

### Theory 6: Balance Sheet Fiction

Hagenauer's balance sheet needed to show the fiction of a large receivable against Brisen. An unsubmitted Schlussrechnung allows the company to book any number it wishes as "Forderungen aus Lieferungen und Leistungen" (trade receivables). Submitting the actual Schlussrechnung would force the receivable to be stated at a defensible figure -- potentially zero.

### Significance for E+H Discussion

The absence of a Schlussrechnung is one of Brisen's strongest arguments in any proceedings. It demonstrates that Hagenauer could not or would not commit to verifiable numbers. The insolvency administrator will have to address this gap. Brisen should proactively raise this point in the Forderungsanmeldung and in any correspondence with the administrator.

### 6.3 SECTION 1330 ABGB COUNTER-ATTACK

Christine Saehn's letter of 27 March -- naming the Vallens personally as "faktische Geschaeftsfuehrer" and attributing insolvency causation to their conduct, sent to dozens of subcontractors -- constitutes prima facie Kreditschaedigung (credit damage) under Section 1330(2) ABGB.

**Elements:**
- **False statement of fact** -- the Vallens are not entered in the Firmenbuch as Geschaeftsfuehrer; E+H has categorically rejected the characterization; the characterization has not been adjudicated by any court
- **Dissemination to third parties** -- the letter was sent to all Hagenauer subcontractors, i.e., dozens of business counterparties
- **Concrete damage** -- Brisen needs these subcontractors for completion works; the letter directly undermines Brisen's ability to contract with them
- **Causal nexus** -- the damage flows directly from the dissemination of the false characterization

**Available remedies:**
| Remedy | Description | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Einstweilige Verfuegung** (preliminary injunction) | Court order prohibiting Saehn/Hagenauer from repeating the faktischer Geschaeftsfuehrer allegation in communications with third parties | Obtainable within days to weeks |
| **Widerrufsanspruch** (retraction) | Demand that Saehn send a retraction letter to all recipients of the 27 March letter | Can be combined with injunction |
| **Schadenersatz** (damages) | Compensation for actual damage caused by the false statements | Longer-term proceeding |
| **Unterlassungsklage** (cease-and-desist action) | Permanent injunction | Main proceedings |

**STRATEGIC VALUE:** Filing for an einstweilige Verfuegung flips the narrative. Instead of defending against the faktischer Geschaeftsfuehrer allegation, Brisen (through the Vallens' personal counsel) is attacking the dissemination of false statements. This puts Nemetschke/Saehn on the back foot and signals to the insolvency administrator that the Vallens will fight aggressively.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Personal counsel should file the einstweilige Verfuegung within 2-3 weeks. The Section 1330(2) claim is strong: the statement is objectively unproven, it was disseminated widely, and the damage is concrete and immediate.
