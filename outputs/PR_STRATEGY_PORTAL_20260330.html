1. The Narrative -- Strengthened by Chronology

The complete chronological analysis of the Hagenauer dispute -- drawing on contracts, meeting transcripts, emails, corporate registry data, and financial records -- has fundamentally strengthened Brisen's position. The story is no longer a bilateral developer-contractor dispute. It is the story of a structurally overcommitted contractor with EUR 25,000 in holding capital who ran a EUR 100M+ construction empire across seven countries, lost millions on a failed tourism venture, created asset-protection vehicles as the walls closed in, and then blamed his largest client for his own collapse.

2. The Corporate Empire

Martin Hagenauer's business spans 15+ entities across 7 jurisdictions (Germany, Austria, Poland, Switzerland, France, Netherlands, and ongoing). The parent company -- Hagenauer Holding GmbH -- has share capital of EUR 25,000. This entity controls approximately EUR 100M in annual revenue. The structural mismatch between capitalisation and exposure is the root cause of the insolvency, not any action by Brisen.

3. The Asset-Protection Pattern

Two entities were created in the years immediately preceding the insolvency:

  • Hagenauer Landwirtschaft GmbH (agriculture/farm holdings) -- created 2022, connected to the 200+ year Hagenauer family farm
  • Hagenauer VV GmbH (residential property management) -- created 2023

Both were established while EUR 100M+ construction projects were running and financial pressure was mounting from the Gruenten failure. These are potential Insolvenzanfechtung targets for the insolvency administrator.

4. The Gruenten Bergwelt Failure (EUR 30M)

Hagenauer acquired Gruentenlifte from insolvency and planned a EUR 30M tourism development. The project was killed in January 2023 when the Alpgenossenschaft refused a land mortgage after 70,000 petition signatures and 2,000+ formal objections. Estimated sunk cost: EUR 2-5M. This failure occurred during the construction phase of the MO Vienna project -- directly contributing to Hagenauer's financial distress.

5. The Hamburg Collapse

Campus Schluetersstrasse Hamburg -- a EUR 144M project for Bayerische Versorgungskammer (Germany's largest public pension fund) -- entered Schutzschirmverfahren on the same day as the Austrian filing (16 March). Hagenauer was running TWO EUR 140M+ projects simultaneously on approximately EUR 100M group revenue. This is not a Brisen-specific failure. It is a group-wide structural collapse.

6. Dominik Buhl's Departure

Buhl was Hagenauer's co-Managing Director since 2016, handling all commercial and strategic matters. He departed on 24 February 2026 -- three weeks before insolvency. He is now a Junior Data Engineer at Der Spiegel. The commercial brain of the business exited when he saw what was coming.

7. The Self-Reduction

Hagenauer's Section 1170b demand was EUR 13.2M. After Brisen presented its counter-calculations, Hagenauer's own lawyers reduced this to EUR 5.85M -- a 55% cut. The original demand was never genuine. It was a manufactured trigger for contract termination.

8. The Real Numbers

ClaimsMax analysis (9 March 2026) shows the actual outstanding amount is EUR 222,000 (excluding EUR 1.8M retention). Compare this to the EUR 13.2M demand. Brisen has paid approximately EUR 114M of the contract value. The gap between rhetoric and reality is extraordinary.
